Distillation Column Design Project

Project Overview

This comprehensive design project involved creating a complete distillation system for separating a 40% ethanol/60% water mixture into 95% pure ethanol and 99% pure water streams. The project spanned 8 weeks as part of my second-year Process Calculations course.

Key Components

  • Determined optimal feed stage location using McCabe-Thiele method
  • Calculated minimum reflux ratio (Rmin = 1.2) and operating reflux ratio (R = 1.5 Rmin)
  • Designed a 20-tray sieve plate column with 0.5m diameter
  • Specified a thermosiphon reboiler and partial condenser system
  • Performed energy balance calculations for heating/cooling requirements
  • Simulated the process using ASPEN Plus for validation

Outcomes

The final design achieved 98.5% ethanol recovery with an energy efficiency of 82% compared to theoretical minimum. Received an A grade and was selected by faculty as an exemplary student project.

Chemical Plant Safety Analysis

Project Overview

A comprehensive hazard and operability (HAZOP) study conducted on a simulated ammonia production plant during my first-year Introduction to Chemical Engineering course. This project introduced me to process safety fundamentals.

Methodology

  • Identified 8 key nodes in the ammonia synthesis loop
  • Applied HAZOP methodology with 7 guide words (No, More, Less, etc.)
  • Identified 23 potential hazards including high-pressure risks and toxic releases
  • Recommended 15 safety measures including pressure relief systems and gas detectors
  • Developed an emergency response protocol for ammonia leaks

Outcomes

The final report was praised for its thoroughness and practical recommendations. The project received a B+ and sparked my ongoing interest in process safety engineering.
